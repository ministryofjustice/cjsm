Benefits of CJSM

CJSM enables you to share sensitive information, up to and including anything marked restricted, with anyone in the criminal justice community, quickly and reliably. So everyday processes can be handled in a more secure, efficient and cost-effective way.

Security benefits

Protection from hackers

The CJSM service encrypts all the information that's sent across it. This means that even in the unlikely event that someone does manage to successfully hack-in, they still won't be able to read any of the information that's been sent or received via CJSM.

CJSM Closed Community can stop Spam

With normal email, you can receive emails from any organisation or person that you haven't 'blocked' from sending to you. CJSM works the other way round - you can only receive emails from addresses that are part of a 'closed community' of users. Unless a sender has been pre-approved, they won't be able to send or receive CJSMs, effectively putting an end to junk email.

How CJSM can help: Every user is individually authenticated

Once an organisation or individual has been given a CJSM account, you can be sure that they and their systems have been authenticated by OCJR's rigorous enrolment process.

That means you have peace of mind that any information you send via CJSM will stay safe and secure.

How CJSM can help with its proceedures:

Before anyone can join the CJSM service, they have to agree to our terms and conditions, which sets out in great detail the levels of security we expect them to maintain.

This ranges from ensuring that they have a hardware firewall installed, to making sure that strangers are accompanied at all times in areas where CJSM is being used.

We also require users to comply with all relevant legislation, including the Data Protection Act, when sending, receiving or storing CJSMs.

How CJSM can help: Virus checking

CJSM uses the very latest virus-checking technology to prevent any viruses from getting through. The virus-checking software is constantly (and automatically) updated, stopping even the newest computer viruses dead in their tracks, before they can infect CJSM users' computers.
